Research summary: platforms, ad techniques, and wording best practices for Atlantic Canada trades recruitment.

What Makes a High-Performing Construction Ad

✅ Lead with the differentiators, not the duties. Tradespeople don't want another job description — they want to know why this job is different.
  • Show pay upfront — ads with salary get 30–40% more applicants in trades; "competitive salary" kills applications
  • Speak to career growth — tradespeople are hungry for it and rarely offered it; management paths are a huge hook
  • Year-round stability — seasonal layoffs are a constant fear; emphasize this hard
  • Short punchy bullets — most trades workers skim on a phone; dense paragraphs lose them fast
  • Plain language — no corporate buzzwords, no "dynamic team player" nonsense
  • Strong "why us" before "what we need" — flip the typical job ad structure
  • Mobile-first — the majority of tradespeople apply via phone
❌ Avoid: Long generic duty lists · "Competitive salary" without a number · Vague culture statements · Excessive requirements that filter good people out

Posting Tips

  • Facebook Groups to target: "Halifax Jobs," "Nova Scotia Trades & Construction," "Halifax Construction Workers"
  • Best posting time: Tuesday–Thursday morning — highest applicant response in trades
  • LinkedIn title hack: Put the pay range in the job title: "Crew Leader — $35–48/hr + Profit Sharing"
  • Boost Facebook posts for $20–30, targeted to men 25–45 within 50km of Halifax, interests: construction/trades
  • Refresh every 2 weeks — stale posts get buried by algorithms; repost to stay visible

Referral Program Recommendation

In trades, the best hires come from within existing crews. Consider a structured referral bonus: $500 on hire, $500 after 90 days. Announce it at a crew meeting and post it in the break area. Referrals in construction carry an implicit quality endorsement — tradespeople don't recommend bad workers to their own crew.
